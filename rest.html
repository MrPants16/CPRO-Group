Resting

Resting is an essential to maintain a positive and healthy lifestyle. Without it, you are at risk of having an inability to regulating your emotions, lack in cognitive ability and it can have physical and mental effects on your health.

To ensure you are resting enough to maintain good health, you should have a consistent sleep schedule and ensure your sleep environmet is dark, cool and comfortable.

Sleep Phases

There are 5 sleep phases. The first is stage 1, it is the traditional stage where you start to sleep, marked by slight drowsiness, slowing down of eye movements and very minimal movement of the muscles. During stage 2, you are just slightly deeper in sleep than you were in stage 1. Stage 2 differs in that you are slowing down your rate of your heart and that you are breathing much slower and your body temperature transfers to a colder state. State 3 is considered "deep sleep" and is THE MOST crucial part of sleeping for an individual to endure. In this phase, the person is not as easy to wake up. Their brain waves are very slow. The 4th state is almost identical to that of the 3rd stage. The 5th stage of sleeping, known as the REM stage is when vivid dreams are likliest to occur. Your brain waves are giving off more activity and your muscles are a lot more relaxed. Fun fact: the average person does this about 5 times every night when they sleep.

Benefits from adequate sleep each night.

  1. Enhanced brain function.
  2. Stronger immune system.
  3. Assists in cardiovascular health.
  4. Reduces the risk of chronic diseases.
  5. Increased energy and you are more alert.

Negative impacts that lack of sleep can have on your life.

  • Increased risk of hypertension.
  • Increased risk of diabetes.
  • Increased risk of depression.
  • Higher blood pressure.
  • Have less energy and motivation to do anything. Feeling more drowsy.
