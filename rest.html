Summary


Resting is an essential to maintain a positive and healthy lifestyle. Without it, you are at risk of having an inability to regulating your emotions, lack in cognitive ability and it can have physical and mental effects on your health.

Ages Recommended Sleep Negative Consequences
1-2 11-14 hours Struggling with learning and may become irritable fast.
3-5 10-13 hours Lack of focus and could have difficulty managing emotions.
6-12 9-12 hours Might cause moodiness and trouble concentrating. Severe lack could lead to anxiety.
13-18 8-10 hours Irritability and a higher risk of depression and anxiety.
19-64 7-9 hours Reduced coordination and increased risk of chronic illnesses.
65+ 7-9 hours Lack of cognitive ability and confusion, as well as increased risk of chronic illness.

Healthy sleep habits


There are many many good sleep habits that will help you sleep more soundly and happily.

  1. Listening to music (or rain sounds, wind, etc.)


  2. Creating and maintaining a healthy and productiv sleep routine or set of steps.


  3. Having a cool, and comfortable sleep environment. Science shows that cooler temperatures actually assist in sleep preservation.


  4. Avoid drinking or consuming any type of alcohol, caffeine or other sleep disrupting things like that.

Improves memory and learning

During sleep, your brain processes and consolidates information from the day, strengthening neural connections. This helps you remember what you studied or experienced.

Boosts Physical Recovery

Deep sleep releases growth hormone, which repairs muscles, tissues, and cells—crucial for athletes or anyone who exercises.

Enhances Mood and Emotional Health

Adequate sleep balances neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine, reducing stress, anxiety, and irritability

Strengthens the Immune System

While you sleep, your body produces infection-fighting proteins called cytokines. Without enough sleep, your body’s defense against illness weakens.

Increases Focus and Productivity

Sleep helps your brain stay alert and make better decisions. Lack of it leads to slower thinking and poor concentration

Supports Heart Health

Sleep regulates blood pressure and reduces inflammation, both of which lower your risk of heart disease and stroke.
