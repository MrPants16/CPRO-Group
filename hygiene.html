The Importance of Personal Hygiene

A man who possesses genius is insufferable unless he also possesses at least two other things: graditude and cleanliness. - Friedrich Nietzsche


Having "good hygiene" is a very broad and often misunderstood topic. At first glance, many individuals see it as very simple actions such as washing your hands properly and showering. However, the concept of exceptional personal hygiene is derived from good internal and external practices.


If someone does not properly maintain themselves, there is a large list of downsides. It allows the body to have an ideal environment for germs and bacteria to grow into possibly much worse infections. On the social level, people may be drawn away from you, leading to a sense of loneliness.

With that being said, personal hygiene actually has several layers and types to it. Most of these have to do with different areas of the human body.



Types of Personal Hygiene

Dental Hygiene

Having a good sense of dental hygiene is key to a good first impression. Practicing simple habits such as brushing 2 times per day as well as extras such as flossing and using mouth wash can drastically reduce the risk of gum disease and cavities while also blocking bad breath.
