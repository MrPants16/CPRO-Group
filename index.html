Welcome to Fatherly Advice! (patent pending)

Fatherly Advice! (patent pendig) is a small and quick collective of helpful tips that we believe everyone in our field could benefit from! Regardless of their current health.

Working in the technical field, whichever branch we end up in, tends to leave us in a rather stagnant work environment. What we mean by this is, we may more often than not find that we are spending hours upon hour sitting at our desks. Putting in hour after hour with less than ideal posture as we crinkle our brow, upon reading the most recent bug report. Or or perhaps we find ourselves so engrossed in our task that we forget to move, or eat, for hours on end as the creative flow takes over.

Though we may not realize it, in both of these scenarios we are setting ourselves up for failure where personal health and accountability is concerned. This can very easily end up with us forming bad habits over time, that become painstakingly
difficult to break. And from the bottom of our hearts, we mean it when we say that we wouldn't want that for anyone. We hope that through the use of our "beginners guide to health" we have formulated here, that everyone
is able to start considering their health with greater importance and take steps towards living a healthier lifestyle! (We are no exception to this either.....)

Nutrition

When considering the steps we can actively take to work on and improve our health, Nutrition is simultaneously the easiest, and most difficult, change to put to practice.
Considering both sides of that double edged sword; Nutrition is extremely easy to put into practice, as it can be as simple as watching, and changing the products that we consume
on a daily basis. Ironcially, this is also how Nutrition can be the most difficult. It's not an easy ask to have you continuously be mindful of what you consume, or to keep on
top of meal prepping / cooking at home instead of eating out or instant meals.

Thankfully, there are plenty of rescources available to help in building these habits, and improving our nutrition. If you're interested in learning more about this,
please visit our nutrition page to learn more!
